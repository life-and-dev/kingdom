# The Roles of Men

> Therefore, in order that faith may find a firm basis for salvation in Christ, and thus rest in him, this principle must be laid down: the office enjoined upon Christ by the Father consists of three parts. For he was given to be **prophet, king, and priest**. -- [Calvin, Institutes 2:15:1](https://thegenevanfoundation.com/munis-triplex-the-offices-of-christ/)

John Calvin was the first to identify the 3 offices of Christ as: prophet, king and priest. Although John himself limited these offices only to Christ, during the Reformation that theologians began applying these roles to laypeople based on Paul's statement:

> **The husband is the head** of the wife even **as [Christ](../../christ.md) is the head of the *church***, [his body](../body.md), and is himself its Savior.
>
> -- Ephesians 5:23 (ESV)

The reasoning is that if "husbands is the head... as Christ is the head" it implies that the same offices hold by Christ should apply to married men too. However, there no single verse in the bible that applies all three these three specific offices directly to individual men. 

The "Prophet, King, Priest" framework is a modern teaching metaphor. It is a useful shorthand to remind men of their diverse responsibilities (teaching, leading, praying). But as a literal biblical doctrine, it does not exist.
