God does not differentiate between nations, status or gender when it comes to salvation.

> For [you are all sons of God](/creation/sons-of-god) through faith in [Christ](/god/son/christ) Jesus. For as many of you as were [baptized](/eternal/saved/baptize) into Christ have put on Christ. There is neither Jew nor Greek, there is neither slave nor free, there is neither male nor female; for you are all none in Christ Jesus. -- Galatians 3:26–28 (NKJV)

> This is the will of Him [God] who sent Me:
>
> that ***everyone*** who sees the Son and [believes](/eternal/saved/belief) in Him may have everlasting life. -- John 6:40 (ESV)

> For there is no distinction between Jew and Greek; for the same Lord is Lord of all, bestowing his riches on all who call on him. For “**everyone** [who calls on the name of the Lord will be saved](/eternal/saved). -- Romans 10:12-13 (ESV)

> After this I looked, and there before me was a great multitude that no one could count, from ***every nation, tribe, people and language***, standing before the throne and before the Lamb. They were wearing white robes and were holding palm branches in their hands. And they cried out in a loud voice: “[Salvation](/eternal/saved) belongs to our God, who sits on the throne, and to the Lamb.” -- Revelation 7:9-10

> For God does not show favoritism. -- Romans 2:11 (NIV), 1 Timothy 2:4

A possible examples of a black people that got converted in the Bible:

* Moses' wife was a Kushite (Ethiopian) (Numbers 12:1).
* The conversion of the eunuch of Ethiopia (Acts 8:26-38).

Ethiopians in Jeremiah's time seems to have black skins according to Jeremiah 13:23 which compares their skin colour with a leopard's spots.
