# The Resurrection

Will the dead rise again? This question has been asked throughout human history. The Bible provides a consistent answer across both the Old and New Testaments: a resurrection is coming for all people. This article explores what Scripture teaches about the resurrection, who will be raised, and what kind of body awaits those who rise.

Daniel prophesied:

> And many of **those who sleep in the dust of the earth shall awake**,
> 1. some to [everlasting life](judgement.md#the-fate-of-gods-children), and
> 2. some to [shame and everlasting contempt](judgement.md#the-fate-of-the-wicked).
>
> — Daniel 12:2 (ESV)

Jesus taught the same:

> Do not marvel at this, for an hour is coming when **all who are in the tombs** will hear his voice and **come out**,
> 1. those who have done good to [the resurrection of life](judgement.md#the-fate-of-gods-children), and
> 2. those who have done evil to [the resurrection of judgment](judgement.md#the-fate-of-the-wicked).
>
> — John 5:28-29 (ESV)

Paul testified:

> But this I confess to you, that according to the Way, which they call a sect, I worship the God of our fathers, believing everything laid down by the Law and written in the Prophets, having a hope in God, which these men themselves accept, that **there will be a resurrection of both the just and the unjust**.
>
> — Acts 24:14-15 (ESV)

## Explaining the Resurrection

> The same day Sadducees came to him, who say that there is no resurrection, and they asked him a question, saying, "Teacher, Moses said, '[If a man dies](../death.md) having no children, his brother must marry the widow and raise up offspring for his brother.' Now there were seven brothers among us. The first married and died, and having no offspring left his wife to his brother. So too the second and third, down to the seventh. After them all, the woman died. In the resurrection, therefore, of the seven, whose wife will she be? For they all had her."
>
> But Jesus answered them, "You are wrong, because you know neither the Scriptures nor the power of God. For **in the resurrection** they neither marry nor are given in marriage, but are like angels in [heaven](https://eternal.family.net.za/bible/concepts/heaven).
>
> — Matthew 22:23-30 (ESV)

A common misunderstanding is that some believe we will immediately turn into angels and live in heaven when we die. Jesus addressed this directly:

- A resurrection must first take place before the dead can live again.
- The resurrected will not become angels. They will be ***like*** the angels.
- There is nothing in this passage that states the resurrected will instantly live in heaven. Jesus compared resurrected people to the angels who are currently in heaven. Scripture describes the eternal home of God's people as the New Earth (Revelation 21:1-3; 2 Peter 3:13).

## The Resurrection Body

Paul also taught:

> But someone will ask, "How are [the dead](../death.md) raised? With what kind of body do they come?"
>
> You foolish person! What you sow does not come to life unless it dies. And what you sow is not the body that is to be, but a bare kernel, perhaps of wheat or of some other grain.
>
> But God gives it a body as He has chosen, and to each kind of seed its own body. For not all flesh is the same, but there is one kind for humans, another for animals, another for birds, and another for fish. There are heavenly bodies and earthly bodies, but the glory of the heavenly is of one kind, and the glory of the earthly is of another. There is one glory of the sun, and another glory of the moon, and another glory of the stars; for star differs from star in glory.
>
> **So is it with the resurrection of the dead**. What is sown is perishable; what is raised is imperishable. It is sown in dishonor; it is raised in glory. It is sown in weakness; it is raised in power. It is sown a natural body; it is raised a spiritual body. If there is a natural body, there is also a spiritual body.
>
> — 1 Corinthians 15:35-44 (ESV)

## Conclusion

Scripture consistently affirms that [a resurrection awaits both the just and the unjust](#the-resurrection). Those who are raised will not become angels, but [they will be like the angels](#explaining-the-resurrection). The resurrection body will not be the same as the current physical body. [It will be spiritual, imperishable, and raised in glory](#the-resurrection-body).
