Christians refer to the term "bondage" as a state where a person is trapped, enslaved or controlled by demons or an addiction.

In the Bible at Mark 5, we read how Jesus had to cast out "unclean spirits" from such a man.

Christians usually say "deliverance" is the release from a "bondage".

It is also possible to do deliverance on yourself with a simple prayer, because in reality it is still the same God who releases you, not your own power.

However, the model that the first believers in the New Testament of the Bible followed was to ***pray for one another***:

> Therefore, confess your sins to one another and **pray for one another**, that you may be healed. **The prayer of a righteous person has great power as it is working.** -- James 5:16

Jesus also said himself:

> “Truly, truly, I say to you, whoever believes in me will also do the works that I do; and **greater works than these will he do**, because I am going to the Father. Whatever you **ask in my name**, this **I will do**, that the Father may be glorified in the Son. If you **ask me anything in my name**, **I will do it**.” -- John 14:12-14 (ESV)

Sometimes people are scared that they might receive an evil spirit during deliverance.

> If you then, being evil, know how to give good gifts to your children, how much more will your heavenly Father give the Holy Spirit to those who ask Him!” -- Luke 11:13

Some bondages might require renunciations or spiritual warfare to reverse previously spiritual legal rights that you or [an ancestors might have installed](https://biblia.com/bible/esv/deuteronomy/5/9). These too could also be reversed with a simple prayer or repentance if you are aware of it. Of course, you may and should also ask God to reveal any such legals rights that were committed.
