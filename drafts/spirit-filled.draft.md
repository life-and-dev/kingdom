When you were [baptized](/bible/concepts/baptism) in the name ([authority](https://biblehub.com/greek/3686.htm)) of Jesus Christ, you will receive the Holy Spirit.

> And Peter said to them, "Repent and be baptized every one of you in the name of Jesus Christ for the forgiveness of your sins, and you will receive the gift of the Holy Spirit." -- Acts 2:38 (ESV)

Example where the disciples did not receive the Holy Spirit (only baptized in the name of Jesus):

> Now when the apostles at Jerusalem heard that Samaria had received the word of God, they sent to them Peter and John, who came down and prayed for them that they might receive the Holy Spirit, for he had not yet fallen on any of them, but they had only been baptized in the name of the Lord Jesus. Then they laid their hands on them and they received the Holy Spirit. -- Acts 8:14-17

Example where the disciples were only baptized into repentance:

> And it happened, while Apollos was at Corinth, that Paul, having passed through the upper regions, came to Ephesus. And finding some disciples he said to them,
>
> “Did you receive the Holy Spirit when you believed?”<br>
> So they said to him, “We have not so much as heard whether there is a Holy Spirit.”<br>
> And he said to them, “Into what then were you baptized?”<br>
> So they said, “Into John’s baptism.”<br>
> Then Paul said, “John indeed baptized with a baptism of repentance (Mark 1:4), saying to the people that they should believe on Him who would come after him, that is, on Christ Jesus.”
>
> When they heard this, they were baptized in the name of the Lord Jesus. And when Paul had laid hands on them, the Holy Spirit came upon them, and they spoke with tongues and prophesied.
>
> -- Acts 19:1-6

## Purpose of the Holy Spirit

* To guide us not to sin (Romans 8:2)
* He makes it possible to have fellowship with Jesus and God (2 Corinthians 13:14)
