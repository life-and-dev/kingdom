# Commit to God

God clearly does not tolerate [idols](/bible/concepts/idolatry) (Exodus 20:3-4).

> “**Those who regard worthless idols forsake their own Mercy.**
>
> But I will sacrifice to you with the voice of thanksgiving; I will pay what I have vowed.
>
> **Salvation is of the Lord.**”
>
> -- Jonah 2:8-9

Therefore, you need to choose if it is worth putting your [trust](/life/faith) in things that fail, like:

* idols (Exodus 20:3-5, Ezekiel 18:6)
* important people (Psalm 146:3)
* wicked people (Psalm 1:1)
* pleasure (Ecclesiastes 2:1-11, Proverbs 21:17)
* money (Matthew 6:24)

> Choose this day whom you will serve. -- Joshua 24:15

# Trust God

## God exists

> Whoever would draw near to God must believe that He exists... -- Hebrews 11:6 (ESV)

Therefore, it is not possible to be saved if you do not at least believe that God exist. This already rules out Atheism, Universalism, and many other religions. However, unlike the Gnostic reasoning, [to only know that God exist is not enough to save you](/eternal/deceptions).

## Jesus is the Son of God

Note that unlike many [church creeds](/kingdom/church/history) that state you have to belief "Jesus *is* God to be saved", the only biblical requirement is to belief that "Jesus is [**the Son** of God](https://son.ofgod.info)".

> For God so loved the world, that He gave His only Son, that whoever believes in him should not perish but have eternal life. For God did not send His Son into the world to condemn the world, but in order that the world might be saved through him. Whoever believes in him is not condemned, but whoever does not believe is condemned already, because he has not believed in the *name [[authority](/bible/concepts/name)]* of the only **Son of God**. -- John 3:16-18 (ESV)

> Who is it that overcomes the world except the one **who believes that Jesus is the Son of God**? -- 1 John 5:5 (ESV)

## Jesus is the Christ

"[Christ](/god/son/christ)" is the English transliterated word for Messiah which means anointed king, high priest, saviour and prophet of God.

> Now Jesus did many other signs in the presence of the disciples, which are not written in this book; but these are written so that you may **believe that Jesus is the [Christ](/god/son/christ)**, the Son of God, and that by believing **you may have life** in his name. -- John 20:30-31 (ESV)

> Who is the liar but he who denies that **Jesus is the [Christ](/god/son/christ)**? This is the antichrist, he who denies the Father and the Son. No one who denies the Son has the Father. 
> 
> Whoever confesses the Son has the Father also. -- 1 John 2:22-23 (ESV)

## Jesus is the bread of life

> Jesus then said to them, “Truly, truly, I say to you, it was not Moses who gave you [the bread](/bible/metaphors/bread) [from heaven](/bible/idioms), but my Father gives you the true bread from heaven. For **the bread of God is he who comes down from heaven and gives life to the world**.”  
> They said to him, “Sir, give us this bread always.”  
> Jesus said to them, “**I am [the bread of life](/bible/metaphors/bread); whoever comes to me shall not hunger**, and whoever believes in me shall never thirst.”
>
> ...
>
> Jesus answered them, “Do not grumble among yourselves. No one can come to me unless the Father Who sent me draws him. And **I will raise him up** on the last day.
>
> It is written in the Prophets, ‘And **they will all be taught by God**.’
>
> Everyone who has heard and learned from the Father comes to me — not that anyone has seen the Father except he who is from God; he has seen the Father. Truly, truly, I say to you, **whoever believes has eternal life: I am [the bread of life](/bible/metaphors/bread)**. Your fathers ate the manna in the wilderness, and they died. This is the bread that comes down from heaven, so that one may eat of it and not die. I am the living bread that came down from heaven. If anyone eats of this bread, he will live forever. And the bread that I will give for the life of the world is my flesh."
>
> The Jews then disputed among themselves, saying, “How can this man give us his flesh to eat?”
>
> So Jesus said to them, "Truly, truly, I say to you, **unless you eat the flesh of the Son of Man and drink his blood, you have no life in you. Whoever feeds on my flesh and drinks my blood has eternal life**, and **I will raise him up** on the last day."
>
> -- John 6:47-54 (ESV)

I cannot quote the entire book of John here, but in short Jesus states that believers should feed themselves with "Jesus".

Obviously Jesus was not encouraging cannibalism, but commonly the majority of scholars will explain that Jesus meant that people should feed on the *teaching* of Jesus as one needs to feed yourself on food like bread. However, Paul explains that it means "participation in the body of Christ":

> The cup of blessing that we bless, is it not a participation in the blood of Christ?  
> ***The bread that we break, is it not a participation in the body of Christ?***
>
> Because there is **one bread**, we who are many are **one [body](/kingdom/body)**, for we all partake of the **one bread**.
>
> -- 1 Corinthians 10:16-17 (ESV)

This means that believers should not live in isolation, but that believers need each other and should support each other as one need to eat food every day to stay alive.

In other words, you need to daily feed yourself daily with the "bread of life" or you will simply "have no life in you".

In a practical sense the bread is considered the teachings of God which Christ testified because of the quote "they will all be taught by God" (John 6:45), however this may also include praying, encouragement, to rendering skilled services or providing resources like physical food, clothes, money, shelter, or anything else that we need in this world to survive and grow.

## Jesus is Lord

> That if you confess with your mouth, “**Jesus is Lord**,” and believe in your heart that God raised him from the dead, you **will be saved**. -- Romans 10:9

> God longs “that **every tongue should confess** that **Jesus Christ is Lord**” - Philippians 2:11

Note that "[Lord](/god/father/name)" means "master". It is often confused with "the LORD" (usually uppercase) which is the English translation of Hebrew name [יְהוָֽה](/god/father/name#yhvh) which is God's name.

## Jesus is still alive

> If you confess with your mouth Jesus as Lord, and believe in your heart that **God raised him from the dead**, you will be saved. -- Romans 10:9

Jesus' mission would have been a failure if [God did not raise him from the dead](/god/son/essence/as-god/resurrected).

## Jesus is the only way to salvation

> Jesus said: "I am **the way, and the truth, and the life**. **No one** comes to the Father except through me." -- John 14:6 (ESV)

This is also how the first believers in the New Testament understood it:

> There is one God, and **one Mediator between God and men the man Christ Jesus**, who gave himself a ransom for all, to be testified in due time. -- 1 Timothy 2:5 (NKJV)

Jesus also warned:

> Enter by the narrow gate; for wide is the gate and broad is the way that leads to destruction, and there are many who go in by it. Because narrow is the gate and difficult is **the way which leads to life**, and there are few who find it. -- Matthew 7:13-14 (NKJV)

> There is **salvation in no one else**, for there is no other name under heaven given among men by which we must be saved. -- Acts 4:12 (ESV)

### There are no other way to get saved

Laws cannot save:

> If righteousness comes through the law, then Christ died in vain. -- Galatians 2:21 (NKJV)

Good works cannot save:

> For it is by grace you have been saved, through faith – and this not from yourselves, it is the gift of God – not by works, so that no one can boast -- Ephesians 2:8-9 (NIV)

Obedience cannot save:

> No one will be declared righteous in his sight by observing the law; rather, through the law we become conscious of sin. We all fall short of the glory of God. -- Romans 3:23 (NIV)
